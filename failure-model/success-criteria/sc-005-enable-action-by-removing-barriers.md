## Description
Structural, operational, and cognitive barriers that block Platform Engineering change have been identified and are actually being removed, making action easier.

## Rationale
Even when the intent to change exists, teams cannot move if barriers remain in place, such as approval bottlenecks, dependency friction, skill gaps, unclear ownership, or difficult tooling. A change effort without barrier removal adds more ideals while pushing execution burden onto delivery teams.

## Evaluation Perspective
- Are the barriers blocking action understood concretely?
- Is barrier removal reflected in platform offerings or operating changes?
- Has action become easier without depending on extra effort or heroics from users?

## Observable Indicators
- Concrete barriers to adoption have been listed and prioritized
- Existing friction has been reduced and the cost of getting started is lower
- Stakeholders are moving from "we want to, but cannot" to "we can"
