## Description
It is possible to explain consistently what Platform Engineering is trying to change, why that change is necessary, and which initiatives will move it forward.

## Rationale
When the vision is vague, the platform ends up carrying conflicting expectations at once, such as tool delivery, ticket substitution, standardization, and enablement. If strategic direction is not connected to concrete initiatives, decision criteria become unstable and scope spreads outward.

## Evaluation Perspective
- Is the vision defined clearly enough to guide decisions rather than remain abstract?
- Are the initiatives logically connected to the vision?
- Can the platform's ownership boundaries and non-ownership boundaries be explained?

## Observable Indicators
- The platform's purpose and major initiatives are described using consistent language
- Stakeholders can reuse the same direction across different contexts
- Initiative selection and prioritization are explained against the vision
