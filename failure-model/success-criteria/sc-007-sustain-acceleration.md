## Description
The effort does not stop after early wins; instead, learning and trust are connected to the next changes, sustaining momentum and continuity of improvement.

## Rationale
In Platform Engineering, one successful change is rarely enough. If the effort becomes fixed or support disperses after the first wins, the organization falls back toward local optimization. Without sustained acceleration, structural problems are only partially addressed.

## Evaluation Perspective
- Are early wins connected to the next change initiatives?
- Are improvement cadences and learning loops being maintained?
- Is momentum continuing as an operating pattern rather than as personal heroics?

## Observable Indicators
- Follow-on initiatives or expansions are being planned and executed from prior results
- There is an ongoing rhythm of review, improvement, and support
- Change momentum is being reproduced rather than fading as a one-time push
