## Description
Platform Engineering principles and practices are embedded not as temporary initiatives but as part of the organization's decision criteria, responsibility boundaries, operating habits, and expectations.

## Rationale
If change is not embedded into structures, culture, and expectations, it regresses whenever people or projects change. For Platform Engineering to stick, not only the platform offerings but also responsibility models and decision criteria need to become institutionalized.

## Evaluation Perspective
- Are platform principles and responsibility boundaries embedded into normal organizational operations?
- Is the change no longer dependent on specific individuals or temporary sponsors?
- Have the new expectations and decision criteria taken root in a repeatable way?

## Observable Indicators
- Shared expectations and role boundaries around the platform are treated as normal assumptions
- Decision-making and operating rules reflect the post-change model
- The model continues without regression even after key people change
