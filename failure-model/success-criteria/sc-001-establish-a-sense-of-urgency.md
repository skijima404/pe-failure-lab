## Description
The need for change is understood by stakeholders as directly relevant to them, and that understanding leads to priority changes and investment decisions.

## Rationale
Platform Engineering is often treated as a medium- to long-term foundational investment, and without clear urgency it gets pushed aside by day-to-day delivery pressure. If urgency is not connected to economic value or business impact, the effort is likely to be seen as a nice-to-have improvement rather than a necessary transformation, making sustained support harder to secure.

## Evaluation Perspective
- Is urgency expressed in terms of business impact or the cost of inaction?
- Is Platform Engineering work being used as a reason for changing priorities?
- Do stakeholders recognize this as a transformation challenge rather than just an improvement request?

## Observable Indicators
- The cost of maintaining the status quo or the opportunity loss is explicitly stated
- Concrete investment decisions or priority adjustments are being made
- Multiple stakeholders are using a shared language of urgency
