## Failure Mode Statement
The output is not explained in user experience terms, so even useful delivery fails to become a recognizable win because people cannot connect it to a lived success outcome.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the team can describe what was built technically, but does not translate that output into a simple statement of what better experience or easier success it creates for users.

## Mechanism
- The explanation stays at feature, architecture, or implementation level.
- The team does not frame the result as a clear before-and-after experience that users can recognize.
- As a result, the output may be useful, but it does not become legible as a short-term win.

## Countermove
- Explain delivered outputs in terms of what user success becomes easier, faster, safer, or more reliable.
- Require a simple experience-level statement for why the output matters, not only a feature description.
- Treat missing user-experience framing as a win-recognition failure, not only as a communication polish issue.
