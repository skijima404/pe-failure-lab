## Failure Mode Statement
Delivery pressure consumes the attention needed for barrier removal, so people keep choosing build work over the operating work that would make delivery easier later.

## Context
This tends to emerge when people are more motivated by service development, feature delivery, or visible implementation work than by the slower work of clearing systemic friction.

## Mechanism
- Attention is repeatedly pulled toward building, shipping, or helping immediate delivery work.
- Barrier-removal tasks are seen as deferrable because they do not produce immediate visible outputs.
- As a result, the effort keeps reproducing the same friction because it never protects enough attention to remove it.

## Countermove
- Make the cost of deferred barrier removal visible in delivery terms, not only in process terms.
- Review whether people with barrier-removal responsibilities are being consistently pulled into build work.
- Protect recurring attention for operating work even when delivery pressure spikes.
