## Failure Mode Statement
People are too exhausted to carry the next phase, so the effort reaches early success without preserving enough human energy to expand from it.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the initial success consumed too much concentrated effort and the team does not recover, redistribute load, or redesign the work before the next expansion step.

## Mechanism
- The first phase is completed through high exertion, emotional intensity, or repeated overextension.
- The effort assumes people can keep going at roughly the same energy level into the next phase.
- As a result, continued expansion is blocked because contributor capacity has already been spent down.

## Countermove
- Check whether contributors have enough real energy left to support expansion after the first win.
- Rebalance work before starting the next phase instead of assuming momentum alone is sufficient.
- Treat exhaustion as a capacity-design failure, not only as a morale issue.
