## Failure Mode Statement
The team waits passively for people to join instead of deliberately recruiting the specific people needed for the coalition.

## Context
This tends to emerge when coalition-building is treated as an open invitation, announcement, or informal gathering process rather than as a targeted design activity.

## Mechanism
- Invitations are handled as broad calls or lightweight asks rather than as intentional recruitment of needed people.
- The group assumes interested or available people will self-select into the coalition.
- As a result, the coalition reflects who happened to join, not who was actually needed.

## Countermove
- Identify who must be recruited directly rather than relying on passive participation.
- Make coalition invitations role-specific instead of generic.
- Review the actual member set against needed roles before declaring the coalition formed.
