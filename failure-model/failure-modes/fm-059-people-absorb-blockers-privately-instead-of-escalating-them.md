## Failure Mode Statement
People absorb blockers privately instead of escalating them, so resistance is converted into personal effort before it can become a shared problem to solve.

## Context
This tends to emerge when people feel responsible for keeping work moving, but do not feel equally responsible for surfacing friction in a way the system must respond to.

## Mechanism
- Individuals treat blockers as something to endure, workaround, or carry themselves.
- Escalation is delayed because raising the issue feels slower, harder, or less reliable than handling it personally.
- As a result, the organization sees less visible friction than actually exists, while the people doing the absorbing become the hidden shock absorbers.

## Countermove
- Normalize escalation as part of responsible execution rather than as a sign of weakness.
- Make hidden workaround effort visible when reviewing how work actually got through.
- Treat repeated private absorption as evidence of a structural barrier, not only as dedication.
