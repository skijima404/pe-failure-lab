## Failure Mode Statement
Participants do not share a workable definition of Platform Engineering, so they enter the effort with incompatible assumptions about what the work is supposed to be.

## Context
This tends to emerge when people join from different backgrounds and prior experiences, but no common conceptual model is established early enough to align them.

## Mechanism
- People use the same label, but mean different things by Platform Engineering.
- The effort does not force those differing definitions into a shared working model.
- As a result, participants align to different scopes, responsibilities, and expectations from the start.

## Countermove
- Define a repository-specific and effort-specific working definition of Platform Engineering.
- Ask participants to restate what Platform Engineering means in this effort and reconcile mismatches early.
- Treat definitional divergence as an onboarding risk, not as harmless variation.
