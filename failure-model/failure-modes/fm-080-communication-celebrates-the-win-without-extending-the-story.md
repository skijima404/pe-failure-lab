## Failure Mode Statement
Communication celebrates the win without extending the story, so attention gathers around what was achieved but not around what should happen next.

## Context
This tends to emerge when short-term win messaging is strong enough to create recognition, but weak at connecting that recognition to continued movement and expansion.

## Mechanism
- Communication highlights the success event itself more than the next change it is meant to enable.
- Audiences are left with a sense of closure rather than a sense of onward acceleration.
- As a result, the same communication that proves success also encourages the perception that the effort has reached a resting point.

## Countermove
- Pair every win announcement with a clear statement of what the win now enables.
- Use recognition messaging to open the next chapter, not only to close the current one.
- Check whether audiences are hearing continuation or completion after a win is announced.
