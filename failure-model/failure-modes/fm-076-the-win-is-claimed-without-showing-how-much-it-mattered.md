## Failure Mode Statement
The win is claimed without showing how much it mattered, so people can hear that something improved without understanding how meaningful the improvement actually was.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the team can point to a useful result, but does not make the scale, significance, or practical weight of that result visible enough for others to judge it.

## Mechanism
- Improvement is asserted qualitatively without enough indication of magnitude or importance.
- Recipients are left to guess whether the result was marginal, substantial, or strategically meaningful.
- As a result, the claimed win remains too soft to function as strong evidence.

## Countermove
- Show not only that something improved, but how much and why that degree of change matters.
- Connect the scale of the result to a recognizable user, operational, or business consequence.
- Treat vague benefit claims as evidence gaps rather than as communication polish issues.
