## Failure Mode Statement
Groups that are essential to the value stream, delivery path, or decision path are treated as stakeholders to manage from the outside rather than as participants who should be represented inside the coalition.

## Context
This tends to emerge when developers, security, delivery, project management, or leadership-adjacent people are acknowledged as affected stakeholders, but are not considered necessary members of the coalition itself.

## Mechanism
- The team distinguishes too sharply between "the coalition" and "stakeholders," and leaves key groups outside the coalition boundary.
- Participation is limited to the core technical group while others are expected to react, approve, or cooperate later.
- The coalition therefore lacks people who can speak from the development-side value stream or explain the necessity of change from a higher organizational vantage point.
- As a result, the coalition lacks the internal bridges needed to move through the value stream and influence broader execution.

## Countermove
- Ask which stakeholder groups must be represented inside the coalition rather than only informed from outside it.
- Review whether the coalition contains people from the teams that will need to change behavior, not only the team initiating the effort.
- Include people who can explain both local delivery impact and broader organizational necessity.
- Treat missing representation from critical stakeholder groups as a coalition design defect.
