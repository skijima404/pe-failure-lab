## Failure Mode Statement
The vision relies on buzzword appeal, idealized aspiration, or conceptual excitement instead of making its relevance clear to execution members.

## Context
This tends to emerge when advocates are energized by the idea of Platform Engineering itself and assume that the same excitement will naturally transfer to people doing the work.

## Mechanism
- The vision is presented in fashionable or idealized language rather than in execution-relevant terms.
- Participants hear something that sounds modern or inspiring, but not something that explains why their own work should change.
- As a result, the vision may generate nodding agreement without producing operational commitment.

## Countermove
- Ask what an execution member would actually gain, lose, or do differently if this vision were real.
- Replace buzzword-heavy framing with language tied to concrete execution problems and choices.
- Test whether people can restate the vision in terms of their own work rather than in generic slogans.
