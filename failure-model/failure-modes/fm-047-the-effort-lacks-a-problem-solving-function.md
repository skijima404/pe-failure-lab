## Failure Mode Statement
The effort lacks a problem-solving function, so no role or group is actively responsible for driving barrier resolution across teams and over time.

## Context
This tends to emerge when barriers are visible, but there is no equivalent of a LACE, Scrum Master, or other coordinating function that keeps the work of resolution moving.

## Mechanism
- Barriers are noticed, but no one is responsible for turning them into a sustained cross-team resolution process.
- The work of follow-up, coordination, escalation, and closure is left diffuse.
- As a result, blockers remain known without being driven to real resolution.

## Countermove
- Define an explicit problem-solving function for barrier resolution rather than assuming it will emerge informally.
- Assign responsibility for keeping blockers visible, coordinated, and moving toward closure.
- Treat missing coordination for barrier resolution as a structural design gap.
