## Failure Mode Statement
The team overreacts to executive language without strategic translation, so leadership phrases or pressure signals distort execution faster than the strategy can absorb them.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the team hears management language as a command to redirect immediately, rather than translating that language back through the existing vision, strategy, and boundary model.

## Mechanism
- Executive wording or pressure is taken literally at the delivery layer without enough interpretation.
- The team changes direction in response to phrases, themes, or requests before checking whether they fit the strategic path.
- As a result, expectations drift because leadership signals are converted directly into operating commitments.

## Countermove
- Translate executive language through the existing strategy before changing execution behavior.
- Ask what a leadership statement changes in principle versus what it changes operationally.
- Treat immediate redirection based on language alone as an expectation-control failure.
