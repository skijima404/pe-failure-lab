## Failure Mode Statement
The strategy omits organizational change work, so the initiative path is defined without explicit phases for adoption, communication, expectation shaping, or operating-model transition.

## Context
This tends to emerge when strategy is treated mainly as a technology roadmap and the team assumes organizational uptake will happen naturally once the technical work exists.

## Mechanism
- The initiative path includes technology work, but not the change work needed for people and teams to adopt it.
- Adoption, communication, and expectation-shaping are treated as secondary or left implicit.
- As a result, the strategy cannot credibly explain how the organization will actually move from current behavior to the intended future state.

## Countermove
- Include organizational change work as explicit phases or initiatives in the strategy.
- Ask how adoption, communication, expectation shaping, and operating-model change will happen, not only what will be built.
- Reject strategy paths that assume technical delivery alone will create organizational movement.
