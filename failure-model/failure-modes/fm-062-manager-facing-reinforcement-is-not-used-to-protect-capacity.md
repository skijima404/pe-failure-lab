## Failure Mode Statement
Manager-facing reinforcement is not used to protect capacity, so the effort fails to keep line managers attentive enough to preserve time, legitimacy, and operating room for barrier-removal work.

## Context
This tends to emerge when change agents and coalition members focus on doing the work itself, but do not actively maintain manager awareness of why the work matters, what value it is creating, and why participation should continue to be protected.

## Mechanism
- Managers are not given regular signals that barrier-removal work is valuable, visible, and worth protecting.
- Positive feedback, external recognition, or symbolic reinforcement is not carried back to the managers who control workload and local prioritization.
- As a result, members become easier to reassign, overload, or deprioritize because their barrier-removal work is not being actively legitimized upward.

## Countermove
- Use manager-facing communication as part of barrier removal, not only as general reporting.
- Feed recognition and concrete value signals back to line managers so they have reasons to keep protecting contributor capacity.
- Treat loss of manager attention as a leading indicator that barrier-removal capacity will be eroded by new assignments or competing work.
