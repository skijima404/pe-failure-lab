## Failure Mode Statement
The change case is discussed mainly as a future-state aspiration or future risk, and the conversation never establishes a present-day issue that makes action necessary now.

## Context
This tends to emerge when participants are comfortable talking about long-term architecture, strategic direction, or industry evolution, but do not anchor the discussion in current organizational pain.

## Mechanism
- The conversation emphasizes what the organization should become rather than what current issue it must address.
- Future downside or future opportunity is treated as sufficient motivation, even when no present loss is made concrete.
- As a result, stakeholders hear a reasonable vision but not a burning platform that justifies current tradeoffs.

## Countermove
- Ask what present-day loss, friction, or risk already exists today.
- Require the group to state why action is needed now, not only why it might matter later.
- Convert future-oriented claims into current cost of inaction before discussing strategy or target state.
