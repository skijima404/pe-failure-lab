## Failure Mode Statement
Adoption KPIs are not designed, so the effort lacks an agreed way to observe whether usage is actually spreading, sticking, or declining.

## Context
This tends to emerge when adoption is considered important, but the team does not turn that importance into explicit indicators of reach, retention, behavior change, or usage depth.

## Mechanism
- The effort proceeds without clear measures for what adoption should look like when it is healthy.
- Teams talk about usage in general terms without shared KPI definitions that make progress legible.
- As a result, adoption remains hard to track consistently or compare over time.

## Countermove
- Define a small set of adoption KPIs that reflect reach, retention, and meaningful use.
- Treat missing adoption indicators as a design gap, not only as a reporting gap.
- Ask how the team would know whether adoption is really growing, stalling, or regressing.
