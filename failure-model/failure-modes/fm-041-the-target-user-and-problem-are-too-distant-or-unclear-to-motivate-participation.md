## Failure Mode Statement
The target user and problem are too distant or unclear to motivate participation, so people struggle to believe in the effort strongly enough to volunteer real energy.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the effort is aimed at users or value paths that feel remote from the participants' own experience, or when the user definition itself remains vague.

## Mechanism
- Participants are asked to support change for a user or problem they do not understand clearly or do not feel close to.
- User definition and value path remain too fuzzy to create confident identification with the effort.
- As a result, people may participate politely, but belief stays shallow because they cannot see the human or operational reality they are supposed to care about.

## Countermove
- Define the user and problem concretely enough that participants can recognize who is being helped and why it matters.
- Check whether the people being recruited can explain the target user and value path in their own words.
- Treat vague or distant user framing as a motivation risk, especially when the effort depends on voluntary support.
