## Failure Mode Statement
The team is too busy to surface and process barriers, so the urgency of ongoing work crowds out the attention needed to detect blockers properly.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the effort is under pressure and people stay locked in delivery or response mode long enough that reflection and barrier processing feel like luxuries.

## Mechanism
- People stay focused on immediate work and do not create space to inspect what is blocking them.
- Barrier surfacing is postponed repeatedly because the team feels it must keep pushing forward.
- As a result, the effort becomes busier while becoming less capable of detecting its own structural friction.

## Countermove
- Protect time for surfacing and processing barriers even during periods of high pressure.
- Treat the inability to reflect as a warning sign that detection capacity is already failing.
- Escalate when delivery pressure prevents the team from maintaining basic barrier visibility.
