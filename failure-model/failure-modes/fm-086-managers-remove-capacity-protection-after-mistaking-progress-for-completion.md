## Failure Mode Statement
Managers remove capacity protection after mistaking progress for completion, so the resources that enabled the first success are withdrawn just when expansion should begin.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the first visible win is interpreted by line managers as the end of the special effort, rather than as proof that the effort should continue and expand.

## Mechanism
- The first win is read as closure instead of as a transition into the next stage.
- Managers stop protecting time, reassign people, or allow new work to fill the space that had supported the effort.
- As a result, continued expansion loses the practical capacity it needed to build on early success.

## Countermove
- Communicate explicitly that the first win is a milestone, not the end of the effort.
- Keep manager attention on what capacity must remain protected for the next phase.
- Treat early removal of capacity protection as a risk to acceleration, not only as a staffing adjustment.
