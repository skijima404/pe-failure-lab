## Failure Mode Statement
The effort has no usable measurement for friction or progress, so barrier signals are not made visible in a durable way.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the team can feel that the effort is struggling, but has no operational measures that make the drag, slowdown, or adoption friction legible.

## Mechanism
- The team does not define measurements that would show whether action is getting easier or harder.
- Barrier detection relies on anecdote, intuition, or memory rather than visible indicators.
- As a result, important blockers stay arguable, easy to dismiss, or hard to prioritize.

## Countermove
- Define a small set of measures that make friction, delay, or adoption difficulty visible.
- Use measurement to complement qualitative reflection, not replace it.
- Treat invisible friction as a detection problem, not only as an execution problem.
