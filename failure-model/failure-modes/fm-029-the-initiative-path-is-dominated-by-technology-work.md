## Failure Mode Statement
The initiative path is dominated by technology work, so the strategy becomes overly centered on building artifacts, platform functions, or project support instead of balancing them with change and value-shaping work.

## Context
This tends to emerge when portals, tooling, platform implementation, or direct project support become the most concrete parts of the plan and displace the less tangible work needed to sustain strategic focus.

## Mechanism
- The visible initiative list is filled mainly with technical delivery items.
- Work such as communication, adoption, expectation control, and value reinforcement is absent or weak compared with build-oriented activities.
- As a result, the strategy looks active but becomes biased toward technology motion that does not by itself sustain focus or remembered value.

## Countermove
- Review whether the initiative path is balanced across technical, organizational, and value-reinforcement work.
- Treat a technology-only path as a warning sign rather than as evidence of strategic completeness.
- Add explicit non-technical work needed to keep the strategy coherent and remembered.
