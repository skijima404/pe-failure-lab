## Failure Mode Statement
The team does not define what should count as a short-term win, so work continues without a clear near-term outcome that would prove progress credibly.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the effort wants visible wins in principle, but never turns that desire into an explicit definition of what result, for whom, and by when should count.

## Mechanism
- The word "win" is used aspirationally rather than operationally.
- The team does not specify what must be delivered, validated, or experienced for a near-term result to count as real progress.
- As a result, people keep working without a stable target for what a short-term win is supposed to demonstrate.

## Countermove
- Define each short-term win as a concrete result with a user, a validation condition, and a time horizon.
- Distinguish between activity completed and a win actually demonstrated.
- Treat undefined wins as planning gaps, not as harmless ambiguity.
