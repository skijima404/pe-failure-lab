## Failure Mode Statement
The vision exists, but the strategy does not, so supporters hear what the effort aspires to become without understanding how it is supposed to move there.

## Context
This tends to emerge when leaders can describe a desirable future, but the volunteer army is not given a usable explanation of the strategy or path behind it.

## Mechanism
- The message explains the destination but not the strategic logic or route.
- Supporters are asked to align to an aspiration without being shown the structure of the effort behind it.
- As a result, people may repeat the vision while remaining unclear on what it means in practice.

## Countermove
- Explain the strategy alongside the vision, not after the fact.
- Ask whether supporters understand not only the destination but also the intended path.
- Reject messaging that inspires without orienting.
