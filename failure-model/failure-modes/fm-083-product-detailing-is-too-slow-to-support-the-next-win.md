## Failure Mode Statement
Product detailing is too slow to support the next win, so the effort cannot turn strategic intent into a sufficiently concrete next result in time.

## Context
This tends to emerge when product-owner-like work, platform product shaping, or the detailed definition of the next user-facing improvement lags behind the pace needed to keep acceleration believable.

## Mechanism
- The next product increment is not detailed clearly enough or early enough to support follow-on delivery.
- Product shaping, prioritization, or definition work becomes the bottleneck between strategic ambition and the next visible win.
- As a result, the organization can imagine more progress in theory, but not see a concrete next step that feels ready to move.

## Countermove
- Pull product detailing forward enough that the next win can be seen as a real path, not only as an intention.
- Treat delayed product shaping as a blocker to acceleration, not only as normal backlog work.
- Ask whether the next win is concrete enough for delivery and recognition to begin forming around it.
