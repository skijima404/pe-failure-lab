## Failure Mode Statement
Volunteer recruitment stays within a narrow functional slice, so critical roles such as developers or other key adopters remain underrepresented.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the effort spreads mainly through one familiar community, such as infrastructure, and does not deliberately recruit from the broader set of roles needed for adoption and influence.

## Mechanism
- Recruitment follows existing team boundaries or comfort zones rather than the actual role mix needed for the change.
- The army grows inside one functional cluster while key practitioner groups remain thin or absent.
- As a result, volunteer participation expands without reaching the roles that would make the change real in day-to-day delivery.

## Countermove
- Review volunteer participation by role, not only by headcount.
- Recruit deliberately from the roles that must adopt, champion, or pressure-test the change.
- Treat missing developer or other critical practitioner involvement as a structural weakness, not as a minor representation gap.
