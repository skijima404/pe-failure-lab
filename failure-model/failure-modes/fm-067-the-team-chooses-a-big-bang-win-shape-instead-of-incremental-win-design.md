## Failure Mode Statement
The team chooses a big-bang win shape instead of incremental win design, so value is withheld until a larger delivery event that takes too long to reach.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the effort assumes that wins only count if they arrive as larger, cleaner, or more complete outcomes rather than as smaller validated steps.

## Mechanism
- Planning favors a more complete end-state over intermediate success points.
- Smaller proof points are discarded because they seem partial, inelegant, or strategically weaker.
- As a result, the first win is delayed until the effort has already spent too long without visible validation.

## Countermove
- Design wins as a sequence of validated increments rather than as one larger reveal.
- Ask what smaller success can be demonstrated honestly without waiting for the full delivery shape.
- Treat big-bang win design as a timing risk for change reinforcement.
