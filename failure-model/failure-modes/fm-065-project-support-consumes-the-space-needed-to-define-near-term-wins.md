## Failure Mode Statement
Project support consumes the space needed to define near-term wins, so the effort stays busy reacting to demand without clarifying what current work is meant to prove.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the team is heavily occupied by support, delivery, or project-facing requests and does not retain enough strategic attention to define a bounded short-term result.

## Mechanism
- Project support work expands without a clear end condition or win hypothesis.
- The team keeps responding to immediate demand instead of drawing a near-term success boundary around the current phase.
- As a result, work continues indefinitely while the definition of a short-term win remains absent or dissolves.

## Countermove
- Put a bounded win hypothesis around support-heavy work before allowing it to dominate the phase.
- Ask what current work is supposed to prove, and what would let the team stop or reframe it.
- Treat endless support without a win definition as a short-term-win design failure.
