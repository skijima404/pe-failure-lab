## Failure Mode Statement
The vision and strategy are not communicated consistently, so people join the effort with different interpretations of what they are supporting.

## Context
This tends to emerge when leaders or organizers adapt the message too freely for different audiences, aim for broad appeal, or leave important parts of the strategy implicit.

## Mechanism
- Explanations vary by audience and become overly accommodating, broad, or ambiguous.
- Because the core message is not stable, participants fill in the blanks with their own expectations and motivations.
- As a result, people believe they are joining the same effort while actually aligning to different imagined versions of it.

## Countermove
- Define the non-negotiable core of the vision and strategy before broad communication begins.
- Check whether the message remains stable across different explanation contexts.
- Treat appealing ambiguity as an alignment risk rather than as a communication success.
