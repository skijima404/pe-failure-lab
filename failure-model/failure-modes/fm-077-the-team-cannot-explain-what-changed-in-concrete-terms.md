## Failure Mode Statement
The team cannot explain what changed in concrete terms, so a claimed win remains disconnected from a clear before-and-after description.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the effort can describe the output delivered, but cannot translate that delivery into a specific statement of what became faster, easier, safer, or more reliable.

## Mechanism
- Evidence focuses on what was built rather than on what changed for users or operations.
- Before-and-after comparison is missing, weak, or left implicit.
- As a result, observers cannot tell what practical shift the win is supposed to prove.

## Countermove
- Require a before-and-after explanation for each claimed win.
- Describe concrete change in terms a user, operator, or decision-maker could recognize.
- Treat inability to state what changed as evidence failure, not only as presentation weakness.
