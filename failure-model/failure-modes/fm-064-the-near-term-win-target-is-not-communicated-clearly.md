## Failure Mode Statement
The near-term win target is not communicated clearly, so contributors do not share a concrete understanding of what the effort is trying to prove next.

## Context
This tends to emerge when leaders or change agents may have an implicit picture of the next desired win, but do not communicate it in a way that shapes shared action and expectation.

## Mechanism
- People are told to make progress, but not what near-term outcome should matter most right now.
- The intended next win remains implicit, scattered, or differently interpreted across participants.
- As a result, work proceeds without a shared frame for what the current phase is trying to achieve.

## Countermove
- State explicitly what the next short-term win is supposed to prove and why it matters now.
- Repeat the near-term target until contributors can explain it consistently in their own words.
- Treat unclear next-win communication as a coordination failure, not only as a messaging issue.
