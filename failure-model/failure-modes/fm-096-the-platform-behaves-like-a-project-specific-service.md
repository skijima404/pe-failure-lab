## Failure Mode Statement
The platform behaves like a project-specific service, so users receive something tailored through engagement rather than something they can access as a repeatable self-service capability.

## Context
This tends to emerge when platform offerings are shaped too much around project-by-project accommodation, making them feel like bespoke services instead of reusable entry points.

## Mechanism
- The service is consumed through negotiated project interaction more than through a standard repeatable path.
- Users learn that getting value depends on engaging the team directly rather than using a shared service model.
- As a result, the platform trains users into dependency instead of self-service adoption.

## Countermove
- Reframe project-specific engagement into standard reusable entry paths wherever possible.
- Ask whether users are learning to consume a service or to request bespoke help.
- Treat project-specific behavior as a blocker to self-service maturity.
