## Failure Mode Statement
Urgency and belief are not translated into the participants' own stakes, so people can hear the message without feeling that it matters to them personally.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the case for change is framed at an executive, organizational, or abstract level, but is not reframed into the local burdens, risks, or gains that would make participation feel self-relevant.

## Mechanism
- The effort explains why the change matters to the organization, but not why it matters to the people being asked to join.
- Participants can understand the message intellectually while still experiencing it as someone else's priority.
- As a result, attendance may happen, but voluntary energy and belief remain weak.

## Countermove
- Ask what concrete burden, leverage, or risk changes for the people being asked to participate.
- Reframe urgency so it answers "Why should this matter to me?" and not only "Why does this matter to the business?"
- Treat weak personal resonance as a motivation design problem, not only as low engagement.
