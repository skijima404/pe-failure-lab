## Failure Mode Statement
The team does not deliberately narrow the service portfolio to a small set of strategically chosen offerings, so effort spreads across too many service candidates at once.

## Context
This tends to emerge when many plausible platform services are visible at the same time, and the team treats broad coverage as progress instead of making a few explicit service bets first.

## Mechanism
- Existing-system support, new-system enablement, data-use services, documentation, templates, architecture help, and related offerings are all treated as near-term candidates at once
- The team does not explicitly decide which service categories should receive concentrated attention first and which should wait
- As a result, product design remains too broad, service investment fragments, and the initiative path fails to build recognizable momentum around a focused set of offerings

## Countermove
- Force an explicit choice of which service categories receive concentrated investment in the current phase
- State which plausible offerings are intentionally deferred, even if they remain strategically attractive
- Treat service portfolio narrowing as part of strategy design, not only as later delivery prioritization
