## Failure Mode Statement
Leadership does not publicly signal that the platform is worth adopting, so broader audiences do not receive enough visible endorsement to treat the new model as important and legitimate.

## Context
This tends to emerge when leaders may approve of the platform privately, but do not visibly frame it as something the organization should recognize and move toward.

## Mechanism
- The platform lacks public message-out from executives or other respected leaders who could reinforce its legitimacy.
- Potential adopters do not hear enough high-level encouragement that the new model is valuable and desirable.
- As a result, adoption remains weaker, quieter, and easier to ignore than it should be.

## Countermove
- Ask leaders to frame publicly why the platform matters and why its adoption is worth pursuing.
- Treat visible endorsement as part of institutionalization, not as optional ceremony.
- Check whether adopters can point to leadership signals that make the platform feel legitimate and worth sticking with.
