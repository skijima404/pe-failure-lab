## Failure Mode Statement
The strategy does not create a firewall against incoming demand that would distort the intended path, so the team is pulled into work that undermines its own strategic direction.

## Context
This tends to emerge when existing organizational pressure, legacy requests, or project-specific needs are allowed to flow directly into the platform team without a strategic buffering mechanism.

## Mechanism
- The team has no explicit rule for what kinds of work it will absorb, redirect, or refuse during the strategic path.
- Legacy or structurally distorting work enters the queue in its original form and reshapes the team's priorities.
- As a result, the initiative path is weakened because the strategy lacks a protective boundary around responsibility and intake.

## Countermove
- Define a strategic firewall for work that should be redirected, transformed, or absorbed elsewhere before reaching the platform team.
- Make "what we will not directly take on" part of the initiative path, not an afterthought.
- Use boundary rules to protect design and sequencing from being overridden by immediate demand.
