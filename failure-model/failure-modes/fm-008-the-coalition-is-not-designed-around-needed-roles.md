## Failure Mode Statement
The coalition is not designed around the roles needed to move change, so critical influence, judgment, and support functions are left uncovered from the start.

## Context
This tends to emerge when people focus on forming a group quickly, but do not first identify which kinds of reach, influence, judgment support, or cross-team connection the coalition must contain.

## Mechanism
- The group does not define which coalition functions need explicit coverage before choosing members.
- People are added without asking what specific role they are meant to play in advancing the change.
- As a result, critical roles remain absent even when a coalition has technically been assembled.

## Countermove
- List the functions the coalition must perform before selecting people.
- Ask of each proposed member: what reach, influence, judgment support, or bridge do they add?
- Treat uncovered roles as coalition design gaps, not as minor staffing details.
