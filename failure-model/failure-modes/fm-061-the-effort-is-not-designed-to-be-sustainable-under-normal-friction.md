## Failure Mode Statement
The effort is not designed to be sustainable under normal friction, so progress depends on people repeatedly spending extra energy to compensate for resistance.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the effort values persistence and goodwill, but does not invest enough in the operating conditions needed for people to keep contributing without exhaustion.

## Mechanism
- Friction is treated as something motivated people should simply push through.
- The system does not adapt fast enough to reduce the recurring cost imposed on contributors.
- As a result, the effort appears to continue for a while, but it becomes progressively harder to sustain because it runs on accumulated strain.

## Countermove
- Design the work so it can continue under ordinary organizational friction without depending on repeated heroics.
- Ask whether the current mode of progress would still hold if people stopped compensating with extra energy.
- Treat contributor exhaustion as evidence that the system is failing to remove enough resistance.
