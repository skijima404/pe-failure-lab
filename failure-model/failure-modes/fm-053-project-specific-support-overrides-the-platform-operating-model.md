## Failure Mode Statement
Project-specific support overrides the platform operating model, so the team is pulled toward DevOps, SRE, or custom project work instead of holding to a reusable platform path.

## Context
This tends to emerge when stakeholders expect direct help for their immediate project problems and the team lacks enough protection to keep those needs from redefining the platform effort.

## Mechanism
- Project-specific requests are accepted faster than the team can redirect them into platform-compatible forms.
- Immediate support pressure is treated as more legitimate than protecting the operating model.
- As a result, the team drifts toward bespoke support and away from reusable platform work.

## Countermove
- Define when project-specific support should be transformed, redirected, or refused.
- Protect the operating model from being rewritten by local urgency.
- Treat repeated project-specific pull as a platform-boundary problem, not only as customer demand.
