## Failure Mode Statement
The team asks for budget, headcount, or executive sponsorship before establishing a clear business frame for why the initiative matters.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the team moves quickly from concept discussion to resourcing requests, assuming that the importance of Platform Engineering will carry the investment conversation on its own.

## Mechanism
- The request for support is made before the current business pain, cost of inaction, or strategic downside is clearly stated.
- Executive stakeholders are asked to commit resources without a decision-ready framing of the issue.
- The resulting hesitation is experienced as lack of support, even though the underlying problem is that the business case was never fully built.

## Countermove
- Separate problem framing from investment asking, and do not combine them in a single premature conversation.
- Make the current loss or executive downside explicit before discussing budget or staffing.
- Treat executive hesitation as a signal to improve reframing, not only as resistance to overcome.
