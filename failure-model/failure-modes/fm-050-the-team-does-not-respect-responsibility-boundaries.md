## Failure Mode Statement
The team does not respect responsibility boundaries, so work repeatedly crosses into areas that should be owned, decided, or escalated elsewhere.

## Context
This tends to emerge when people are motivated to keep things moving, but the effort lacks enough discipline around what should be owned directly, what should be coordinated, and what should be escalated out.

## Mechanism
- Teams step into work outside their responsibility boundary instead of using clearer ownership and escalation paths.
- Because boundaries are not respected, many actions trigger fresh coordination about who should really decide or carry the work.
- As a result, communication cost rises, decision latency increases, and stalled "waiting for the other side" patterns emerge.

## Countermove
- Define and reinforce which work should be owned directly, coordinated jointly, or escalated elsewhere.
- Treat repeated cross-boundary rescue behavior as a design failure rather than as helpful flexibility.
- Use clearer ownership and escalation rules to reduce avoidable coordination churn.
