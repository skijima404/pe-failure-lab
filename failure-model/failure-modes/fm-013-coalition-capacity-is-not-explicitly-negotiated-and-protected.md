## Failure Mode Statement
Coalition capacity is not explicitly negotiated and protected, so participation is treated as something members should do only within whatever time happens to remain.

## Context
This tends to emerge when coalition work is acknowledged as important in principle, but no concrete agreement is made with managers or members about how much real capacity will be protected for it.

## Mechanism
- Members or managers settle on vague agreements such as "do what you can" instead of protected capacity commitments.
- Coalition work is expected to coexist with primary delivery work without active workload rebalancing.
- When actual participation drops below what the coalition needs, the Change Agent does not successfully escalate and force workload adjustment through managers.

## Countermove
- Establish explicit capacity commitments for coalition members rather than informal availability assumptions.
- Involve managers in protecting that capacity before coalition work begins.
- Treat repeated under-allocation as a management issue that requires escalation and workload reordering.
