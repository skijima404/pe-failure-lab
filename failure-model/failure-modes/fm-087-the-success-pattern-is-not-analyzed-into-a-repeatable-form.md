## Failure Mode Statement
The success pattern is not analyzed into a repeatable form, so the effort cannot scale because people do not know what exactly made the initial success work.

## Context
This tends to emerge when an early success is celebrated or reused informally, but the drivers of that success are not broken down into principles, conditions, or practices that others can follow.

## Mechanism
- The effort treats the initial success as something that happened rather than as something to be decomposed and learned from.
- Others are expected to reproduce the outcome without a clear model of what made it work.
- As a result, scaling depends on the original people being present rather than on a transferable success pattern.

## Countermove
- Analyze the first success into conditions, moves, and decisions that others can actually reuse.
- Distinguish between "we succeeded once" and "we understand how to repeat this."
- Treat lack of repeatable learning as a scaling risk, not only as missing documentation.
