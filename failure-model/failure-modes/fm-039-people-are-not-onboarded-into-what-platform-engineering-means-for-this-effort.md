## Failure Mode Statement
People are not onboarded into what Platform Engineering means for this effort, so they do not know what kinds of contribution, scope, or behavior are expected from them.

## Context
This tends to emerge when participation expands faster than conceptual onboarding, or when organizers assume the term Platform Engineering is already self-explanatory.

## Mechanism
- Participants are invited in before they are given a usable explanation of what Platform Engineering means in this specific effort.
- Because the concept remains underexplained, people cannot infer what they are actually supposed to do.
- As a result, work scope drifts because participants fill in the gap with their own assumptions.

## Countermove
- Onboard participants explicitly into what Platform Engineering means in this effort, not only in general.
- Explain the intended scope, contribution modes, and non-goals alongside the concept itself.
- Treat "people do not know what to do" as a signal of conceptual onboarding failure.
