## Failure Mode Statement
The team substitutes outputs, tools, or deliverables for the vision instead of defining the change the platform is meant to create.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the group is more comfortable discussing visible artifacts such as portals, templates, or tooling than articulating the organizational change those things are supposed to enable.

## Mechanism
- When asked for the vision, the conversation shifts to concrete outputs or solution artifacts.
- The group treats a thing to build as if it were strategic direction.
- As a result, people can name deliverables, but not the change those deliverables are meant to create.

## Countermove
- Ask what organizational change would still matter even if the current proposed artifact changed.
- Separate the question "What are we trying to change?" from "What might we build?"
- Reject deliverable-only answers when the discussion is supposed to define the vision.
