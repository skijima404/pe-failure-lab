## Failure Mode Statement
Key operating knowledge remains tacit and person-bound, so the effort depends on a few individuals' memory, judgment, and spoken guidance rather than on shareable operating patterns.

## Context
This tends to emerge when important know-how lives in conversation, habit, or personal interpretation instead of being made explicit enough for others to apply reliably.

## Mechanism
- Critical ways of working are transmitted informally through discussion or personal coaching.
- New or adjacent contributors cannot easily act without access to the original people who hold the method in their heads.
- As a result, the effort stays person-dependent even when others are willing to help or replicate it.

## Countermove
- Make critical operating knowledge explicit enough that others can apply it without relying on oral transmission alone.
- Identify where judgment or method still depends on who is present.
- Treat tacit-only operating knowledge as a scaling blocker, not just as a collaboration style.
