## Failure Mode Statement
The claimed win has too little real improvement effect, so it can be described as a win but does not produce enough actual change to function as convincing evidence.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the team is under pressure to show progress and labels a minor, local, or low-impact improvement as a meaningful short-term win.

## Mechanism
- A result is celebrated before its practical impact is tested against real use or real conditions.
- The improvement exists, but its effect is too small, narrow, or weak to support a strong claim of progress.
- As a result, the effort accumulates wins that sound positive but do not materially strengthen belief or justification.

## Countermove
- Test whether the claimed win creates enough real change to warrant being used as evidence.
- Distinguish between a positive step and a persuasive short-term win.
- Treat low-impact wins as weak reinforcement unless they are framed honestly and proportionately.
