## Failure Mode Statement
The urgency case is explained only through executive goals or management targets, without translating why the issue matters to the people expected to join the guiding coalition.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the team succeeds in speaking upward to leadership but does not adapt the story for peers, adjacent teams, or prospective coalition members.

## Mechanism
- The case is framed in terms such as cost reduction, strategic alignment, or efficiency targets.
- Potential coalition members hear what leadership wants, but not what current burden, risk, or opportunity matters to them.
- As a result, people may understand the objective but still not feel personally motivated to join or support the effort.

## Countermove
- Ask what concrete burden, friction, or opportunity this change creates for each coalition audience.
- Rewrite the urgency case in language that speaks to the lived concerns of the people whose support is needed.
- Test whether potential coalition members can explain why the change matters to them, not only to executives.
