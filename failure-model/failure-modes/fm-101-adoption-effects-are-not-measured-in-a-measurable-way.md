## Failure Mode Statement
Adoption effects are not measured in a measurable way, so claims about usage, improvement, or stickiness cannot be validated reliably.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the team wants to show that adoption is working, but relies on impressionistic, anecdotal, or inconsistent evidence rather than on measurable observation.

## Mechanism
- Effect measurement is attempted, but not through methods that produce durable and comparable evidence.
- Observations about adoption remain soft enough that they can be argued, forgotten, or interpreted differently across audiences.
- As a result, the organization cannot rely on the evidence to steer or defend institutionalization.

## Countermove
- Choose measurable methods for observing adoption effects rather than relying on impression or occasional reporting.
- Distinguish between interesting signals and evidence strong enough to support decisions.
- Treat non-measurable effect claims as observability weakness, not only as incomplete analytics.
