## Failure Mode Statement
The effort has no reliable reflection loop, so barriers are not surfaced through regular retrospection or structured review.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the team is focused on keeping the work moving, but does not create recurring moments to inspect what is actually blocking action.

## Mechanism
- There is no recurring retrospective, review, or reflection practice aimed at surfacing barriers.
- Friction is experienced locally and transiently instead of being turned into a shared signal.
- As a result, blockers remain fragmented, easy to forget, and hard to escalate.

## Countermove
- Create a recurring barrier-focused reflection loop rather than relying on ad hoc complaints.
- Ask explicitly what is slowing action, adoption, or coordination at regular intervals.
- Reserve explicit iteration or time windows for reflection and improvement work instead of expecting them to appear inside delivery pressure.
- Treat missing retrospection as a barrier-detection failure, not as a neutral process choice.
