## Failure Mode Statement
Intermediate communication is too thin to maintain a recognition chain, so recipients cannot connect separate updates into a coherent understanding of progress and value.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the team shares too little between major milestones, leaving audiences without enough continuity to remember why the work mattered or how current results connect to prior signals.

## Mechanism
- Long gaps or weak updates break the narrative continuity of the effort.
- Stakeholders receive isolated fragments rather than a reinforcing sequence that helps them connect progress over time.
- As a result, even real wins feel disconnected, forgettable, or hard to interpret in context.

## Countermove
- Use intermediate communication to preserve continuity of meaning, not only to report status.
- Help recipients connect each update to the larger story of what has already been achieved and what is being proven next.
- Treat forgetting and narrative breakage as risks to win recognition, not only as communication noise.
