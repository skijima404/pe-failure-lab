## Failure Mode Statement
The initiative path defers visible value for too long, so the strategy does not produce reinforcing proof points early enough to stay remembered and supported.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the high-level plan concentrates meaningful wins too far into the future and assumes the organization will sustain belief without earlier evidence of value.

## Mechanism
- The path does not include near-term value that stakeholders can recognize and remember.
- Quick wins are placed too late to reinforce credibility, attention, or strategic memory.
- As a result, support decays before the strategy has produced enough visible proof that it is working.

## Countermove
- Ask what visible value will exist in the near term, not only at the end of a long plan.
- Use high-level sequencing that creates reinforcing proof points early enough to sustain support.
- Treat very distant quick wins as a warning sign that the initiative path is not self-reinforcing.
