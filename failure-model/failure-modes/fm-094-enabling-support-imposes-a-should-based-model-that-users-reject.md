## Failure Mode Statement
Enabling support imposes a should-based model that users reject, so the help intended to increase adoption instead reduces satisfaction and pushes users away.

## Context
This tends to emerge when enabling or coaching activity is delivered as correction, doctrine, or normative pressure rather than as support grounded in the user's current reality and constraints.

## Mechanism
- Support interactions emphasize what users should do more than what helps them succeed in context.
- Users experience the enabling function as judgmental, intrusive, or disconnected from their practical needs.
- As a result, adoption may begin, but satisfaction collapses and users become less willing to stay engaged.

## Countermove
- Ground enabling work in the user's current situation before prescribing better practice.
- Treat rejection of enabling support as feedback about approach, not only as resistance to change.
- Ask whether support is being experienced as helpful partnership or as imposed doctrine.
