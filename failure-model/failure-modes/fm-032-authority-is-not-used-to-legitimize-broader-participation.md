## Failure Mode Statement
Authority is not used to legitimize broader participation, so potential supporters do not receive a clear signal that joining the effort is important, safe, and organizationally supported.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the team tries to grow the volunteer army through enthusiasm or informal networking alone, without visible sponsorship from people who can legitimize participation across the organization.

## Mechanism
- Potential participants are never given a strong signal from leadership or other authority figures that the effort matters and that joining it is acceptable.
- Managers and adjacent stakeholders are left to interpret the effort as optional side activity rather than as a supported part of organizational change.
- As a result, broader participation remains weak or skewed because people do not feel sufficiently authorized to invest time, credibility, or attention.

## Countermove
- Use visible authority to signal that broader participation is wanted and legitimate.
- Ask leaders or respected authority figures to frame why the effort matters and why participation is worth making room for.
- Treat kickoff legitimization and public sponsorship as part of volunteer-army formation, not as optional ceremony.
