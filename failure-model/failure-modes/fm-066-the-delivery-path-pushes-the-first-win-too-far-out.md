## Failure Mode Statement
The delivery path pushes the first win too far out, so the effort goes too long before producing visible proof that it is working.

## Context
This tends to emerge when planning assumes that meaningful value can only be shown after a long chain of work, dependencies, or preparatory delivery has been completed.

## Mechanism
- The first credible win is placed behind too much setup, integration, or coordination work.
- The plan tolerates a long period of invisible effort before anything can be shown as real progress.
- As a result, short-term wins arrive too late to sustain attention, memory, or support.

## Countermove
- Pull the first visible win forward until it lands inside a timeframe that can sustain stakeholder attention.
- Treat long invisible setup phases as a risk to win generation, not only as a delivery reality.
- Ask what proof of progress can be made visible before the larger body of work is complete.
