## Failure Mode Statement
The effort burns out after the first major push, so the people who created the initial success no longer have enough energy to sustain forward acceleration.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the first win was achieved through unusually high exertion, concentrated heroics, or emotionally intense mobilization that cannot be repeated safely.

## Mechanism
- The first success is produced by spending too much energy too quickly.
- Recovery, redistribution, or sustainable continuation is not designed after the initial push.
- As a result, the effort reaches proof once, but lacks the energy base needed to accelerate again.

## Countermove
- Review whether the first win was achieved in a way that can be repeated without exhaustion.
- Reduce dependence on heroic effort before treating the first win as a stable foundation for acceleration.
- Treat burnout after an initial win as an acceleration-design failure, not only as a people issue.
