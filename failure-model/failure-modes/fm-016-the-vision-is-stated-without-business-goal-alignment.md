## Failure Mode Statement
The vision is stated without clearly aligning it to business goals, mission priorities, or outcomes the organization is already trying to achieve.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the team can describe a compelling direction for Platform Engineering, but does not explicitly connect that direction to what the business is measured on, trying to protect, or trying to accelerate.

## Mechanism
- The vision is presented as strategically good in itself rather than as a response to declared business goals or mission priorities.
- The group assumes the organizational relevance is obvious and leaves the alignment implicit.
- As a result, the vision sounds reasonable, but its business necessity remains weak and hard to defend.

## Countermove
- Ask which business goals, mission commitments, or executive priorities the vision is meant to support.
- Make the alignment explicit in the vision explanation rather than leaving it implied.
- Reject strategy language that cannot be tied back to a real organizational objective.
