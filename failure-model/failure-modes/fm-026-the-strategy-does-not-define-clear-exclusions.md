## Failure Mode Statement
The strategy does not define clear exclusions and concentration choices, so the team cannot defend what work must remain outside the main path or only be done in a clearly supporting role.

## Context
This tends to emerge when strategic discussion focuses on attractive possibilities and desired work, but avoids the harder boundary-setting needed to keep the initiative path coherent.

## Mechanism
- The team names initiatives but does not define the corresponding non-goals or exclusions.
- The team does not explicitly choose which services or workstreams deserve concentrated investment first.
- There is no durable explanation for why some demands, requests, or adjacent opportunities should stay outside the path.
- Project-specific work may still happen, but not with a clear strategic explanation of whether it is supporting adoption, signaling value, or merely diverting effort.
- Without explicit exclusions, the team is pulled into project-specific DevOps or SRE support that sits outside the intended platform path.
- As a result, focus weakens because the strategy cannot defend its own boundaries or preserve continuity in the initiative path.

## Countermove
- Define explicit non-goals and exclusions alongside the initiative list.
- Make early concentration choices about which services or workstreams are the current main bets.
- Require the team to explain not only what it will do, but what it will not do and why.
- Require a clear strategic explanation when project-specific work is accepted as supporting activity rather than main-stream work.
- Treat missing exclusions as a strategic defect, not as harmless openness.
