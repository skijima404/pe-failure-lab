## Failure Mode Statement
The new model is not reinforced through active organizational messaging, so adoption remains too quiet and optional to become clearly legible as the emerging norm.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the platform offering exists and can be used, but the organization does not keep signaling clearly that the new way is worth noticing, preferring, and normalizing.

## Mechanism
- The organization puts the new model in place but does not keep messaging that it should be actively used and recognized.
- Users are left to infer the importance of the platform from local experience alone rather than from ongoing organizational reinforcement.
- As a result, adoption stays weakly visible and too reversible to become durable.

## Countermove
- Use active messaging to reinforce that the new model is not just available, but worth preferring and normalizing.
- Keep adoption-oriented communication going beyond launch or initial delivery.
- Treat quiet availability without reinforcement as a durability risk.
