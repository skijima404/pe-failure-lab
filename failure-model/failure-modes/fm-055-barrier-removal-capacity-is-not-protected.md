## Failure Mode Statement
Barrier removal capacity is not protected, so the people expected to clear friction are repeatedly pulled back into delivery and service work.

## Context
This tends to emerge when barrier-removal work is treated as something important but secondary, rather than as a protected operating function with explicit capacity.

## Mechanism
- People are assigned barrier-removal responsibilities without corresponding time protection.
- Delivery, support, or build work is consistently treated as more legitimate than keeping friction off the system.
- As a result, barrier-removal work is the first thing to lose attention when pressure rises.

## Countermove
- Protect explicit capacity for barrier-removal work instead of expecting it to survive informally.
- Make tradeoffs visible when delivery work starts consuming the time reserved for removing blockers.
- Treat loss of barrier-removal capacity as an operating risk, not only as a staffing inconvenience.
