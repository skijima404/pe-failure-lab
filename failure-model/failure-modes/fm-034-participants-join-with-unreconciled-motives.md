## Failure Mode Statement
Participants join with different motives tied to their home teams or local interests, and those motives are not reconciled against the actual vision and strategy.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the volunteer army draws people from different teams, each with their own pressures and hopes, but no effort is made to align those motives to a shared direction.

## Mechanism
- People join for different reasons such as tooling needs, local pain relief, visibility, influence, or general interest.
- The effort does not force participants to reconcile those motives with the stated vision and strategy.
- As a result, the volunteer army grows in membership without becoming strategically aligned.

## Countermove
- Ask participants why they are joining and compare those motives against the stated vision and strategy.
- Make motive alignment an explicit part of onboarding rather than assuming shared intent.
- Treat unresolved local agendas as alignment risks before participation scales further.
