## Failure Mode Statement
People do not escalate problems that exceed their role, so issues that require another owner, authority, or boundary decision remain trapped at the wrong level.

## Context
This tends to emerge when responsibility boundaries are blurry, escalation paths are weak, or people assume they should somehow fix everything that lands near them.

## Mechanism
- Work continues at the local level even when the real fix depends on another team, manager, or decision-maker.
- People stay in problem-solving mode longer than their role can legitimately carry.
- As a result, resistance is absorbed locally instead of being moved to the level where the system can change.

## Countermove
- Make it explicit which kinds of friction must be escalated beyond the local team.
- Distinguish responsible ownership from overreaching into problems that need another authority.
- Review recurring issues for signs that people are holding problems below the level where they can actually be solved.
