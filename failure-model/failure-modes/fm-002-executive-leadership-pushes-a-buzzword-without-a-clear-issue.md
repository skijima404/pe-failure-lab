## Failure Mode Statement
Executive leadership pushes the initiative as a trendy priority without clearly understanding or communicating which concrete business issue it is supposed to solve.

## Context
This tends to emerge when leadership wants to appear current, decisive, or aligned with external trends, but the initiative is not grounded in a clearly defined internal pain, loss, or operational pressure.

## Mechanism
- Leadership introduces the initiative as something the organization should do, without first establishing what issue made it necessary.
- Potential change agents receive the work as a top-down assignment rather than as a response to a clearly understood problem.
- Because the originating issue is not shared in concrete terms, downstream participants cannot restate why the effort started.

## Countermove
- Require leadership to state the current business issue, present loss, or cost of inaction in explicit terms.
- Ask change agents to restate the triggering issue before moving into planning or mobilization.
- Reject initiative framing based only on trend relevance, external credibility, or generic strategic language.
