## Failure Mode Statement
Resolution ownership is not made explicit, so barriers are known without clear accountability for who must remove them.

## Context
This tends to emerge when teams discuss blockers collectively, but stop short of assigning who has the authority and responsibility to resolve each one.

## Mechanism
- Barrier discussion ends without explicit ownership for next action and closure.
- Accountability is assumed, shared vaguely, or left implicit across teams.
- As a result, surfaced barriers remain in circulation without becoming someone's resolved responsibility.

## Countermove
- Assign explicit resolution ownership for every barrier that is being tracked.
- Distinguish between people who notice a barrier and people who own removing it.
- Treat unowned blockers as unresolved by definition.
