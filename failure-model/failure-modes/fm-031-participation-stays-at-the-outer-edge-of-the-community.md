## Failure Mode Statement
Participation stays at the outer edge of the community, so the people who join are mostly observers or information-seekers rather than contributors who will carry the change.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the effort attracts curiosity and ambient interest, but does not convert that attention into deeper participation by people willing to invest effort, credibility, or adoption energy.

## Mechanism
- The people who show up are interested in learning what is happening, but are not stepping into meaningful contribution roles.
- The team treats attendance or light interest as evidence that the volunteer army is forming.
- As a result, apparent participation grows without creating the contributor base needed for real movement.

## Countermove
- Distinguish between observers, casual participants, and contributors when assessing volunteer growth.
- Create explicit paths for people to move from peripheral interest into meaningful contribution.
- Do not count information-seeking attendance as proof that a volunteer army has formed.
