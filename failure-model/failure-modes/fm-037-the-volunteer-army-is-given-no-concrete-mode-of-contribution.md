## Failure Mode Statement
The volunteer army is given no concrete mode of contribution, so people cannot tell what they are actually supposed to do in support of the change.

## Context
This tends to emerge when organizers ask for support at a high level, but do not define what contribution, behavior, or participation is expected from volunteers.

## Mechanism
- The message remains abstract and does not specify how volunteers can help.
- People understand that they are supposed to support the effort, but not what action that support should take.
- As a result, interest does not translate into meaningful contribution or adoption behavior.

## Countermove
- Define concrete participation modes for volunteers rather than asking only for general support.
- Explain what specific actions, behaviors, or advocacy roles are expected.
- Treat abstract support requests as incomplete onboarding.
