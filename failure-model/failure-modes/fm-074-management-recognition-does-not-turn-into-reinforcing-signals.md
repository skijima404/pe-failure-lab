## Failure Mode Statement
Management recognition does not turn into reinforcing signals, so even when leaders notice a win they do not help amplify it through sponsorship, praise, or priority-setting.

## Context
This tends to emerge when management or executive audiences are aware that something useful happened, but do not actively echo that success in ways that strengthen legitimacy, attention, or momentum.

## Mechanism
- Managers or executives recognize the result privately or passively without translating that recognition into visible support.
- Contributors and surrounding teams do not receive reinforcing signals that the win matters organizationally.
- As a result, recognition stays too weak to strengthen belief, memory, or follow-on support.

## Countermove
- Ask leaders not only to notice wins, but to reinforce them visibly through praise, framing, and sponsorship.
- Treat management echo and executive acknowledgment as part of win amplification.
- Check whether recognized wins are producing downstream signals in priority, language, or support.
