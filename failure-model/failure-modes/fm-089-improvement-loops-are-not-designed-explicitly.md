## Failure Mode Statement
Improvement loops are not designed explicitly, so the effort lacks a deliberate recurring mechanism for learning, adjustment, and continued evolution.

## Context
This tends to emerge when people assume that team improvement, product improvement, or learning will happen naturally, without explicitly defining how feedback, review, and change should recur.

## Mechanism
- The effort does not establish a designed cadence or structure for converting experience into recurring improvement.
- Learning and adaptation are treated as desirable habits rather than as operating mechanisms that need to be built.
- As a result, improvement remains accidental, fragile, or absent instead of becoming a durable loop.

## Countermove
- Design explicit loops for how feedback, review, prioritization, and change should recur.
- Distinguish between wanting continuous improvement and having a mechanism that actually produces it.
- Treat missing loop design as an acceleration failure, not only as a process omission.
