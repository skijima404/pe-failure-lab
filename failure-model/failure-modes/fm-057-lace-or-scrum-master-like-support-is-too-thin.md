## Failure Mode Statement
LACE or Scrum-Master-like support is too thin, so the effort lacks enough facilitation and operating attention to keep barrier-removal work moving.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the problem-solving and coordination function exists in name, but is spread too thin, heavily part-time, or dependent on a small number of overloaded people.

## Mechanism
- The roles that should facilitate escalation, coordination, and follow-through are underpowered.
- A few people are expected to hold together retrospectives, issue flow, escalation, and operating discipline on top of other work.
- As a result, barrier-removal work slows down even when the need for it is obvious.

## Countermove
- Assess whether LACE, Scrum-Master-like, or equivalent support has enough real capacity to sustain the operating cadence.
- Reduce the number of critical functions carried by a thin coordinating layer.
- Treat facilitation and barrier-removal support as leverage work, not as optional overhead.
