## Failure Mode Statement
Volunteer participation is skewed toward build work only, so the army attracts people who want to develop technical artifacts while neglecting roles needed for facilitation, product direction, or communication.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the most visible contribution path is hands-on platform or portal development, and other contribution modes are not legitimized or designed explicitly.

## Mechanism
- The effort signals that the valuable way to contribute is to build technical things directly.
- People who could contribute as PO, Scrum Master, communicator, enabler, or coordinator are not actively invited into meaningful roles.
- As a result, the volunteer army grows around technical construction while non-build functions needed for adoption and continuity remain thin.

## Countermove
- Define multiple legitimate contribution modes, not only technical implementation.
- Recruit and name non-build roles explicitly when growing the volunteer army.
- Treat an army made only of builders as incomplete, even if technical energy is high.
