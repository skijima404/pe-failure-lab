## Failure Mode Statement
The coalition follows externally provided methods or playbooks without developing internal authorship over why the work matters, how it should be adapted, or who is truly carrying it.

## Context
This tends to emerge when consultants, external advisors, or imported change methods are treated as the source of direction, and the internal group focuses on faithful execution rather than internal ownership.

## Mechanism
- The coalition treats external guidance as something to follow correctly rather than something to internalize and adapt.
- Members repeat consultant framing or textbook steps without building their own explanation of why this coalition should exist in their context.
- As a result, activity can look disciplined and methodical while real ownership remains outside the coalition.

## Countermove
- Ask coalition members to restate in their own words why this coalition exists and what change it is meant to carry.
- Require internal adaptation of external methods instead of faithful repetition by default.
- Treat consultant guidance as support input, not as the source of coalition identity or momentum.
