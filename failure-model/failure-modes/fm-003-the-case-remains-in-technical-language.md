## Failure Mode Statement
The initiative is explained primarily in technical language, and the conversation never shifts into the business terms executives use to evaluate urgency.

## Context
This tends to emerge when platform advocates are strongest at describing architecture, tooling, and developer experience, but are less prepared to connect those topics to cost, risk, speed, or strategic execution.

## Mechanism
- The discussion stays focused on technical correctness, platform capabilities, or engineering pain.
- Participants assume the business relevance is self-evident and do not explicitly reframe the issue for executive listeners.
- As the conversation remains technical, leadership hears an improvement proposal rather than an urgent business case.

## Countermove
- Require every technical claim to be restated in terms of cost, risk, speed, or strategic impact.
- Ask explicitly what executive concern is damaged if the current situation continues.
- Stop the discussion when the answer to "why leadership should care now" is still technical.
