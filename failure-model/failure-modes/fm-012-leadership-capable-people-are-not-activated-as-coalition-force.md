## Failure Mode Statement
People with leadership presence or political reach are present around the effort, but they are not activated as active coalition force.

## Context
This tends to emerge when influential people remain adjacent to the work as advisors, observers, or symbolic sponsors, while the practical burden of moving the change stays with the Change Agent or core team.

## Mechanism
- Leadership-capable people are visible near the effort, but they do not actively carry coalition weight in difficult decisions or influence work.
- The Change Agent continues to do upward influence, resistance handling, and cross-boundary persuasion that should have been shared.
- As a result, the coalition appears to exist, but its real political and leadership weight remains too weak and too concentrated in the wrong place.

## Countermove
- Ask which coalition members are expected to carry leadership weight in moments of resistance or trade-off.
- Convert symbolic support into explicit coalition responsibility and visible action.
- Treat repeated dependence on the Change Agent for political work as a coalition design failure.
