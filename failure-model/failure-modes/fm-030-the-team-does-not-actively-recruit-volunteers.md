## Failure Mode Statement
The team does not actively recruit volunteers, so broader participation is left to chance instead of being intentionally built.

## Context
This tends to emerge when spreading the change is treated as something that will happen naturally once the idea exists, rather than as a deliberate mobilization activity.

## Mechanism
- The team does not run explicit outreach, invitation, or recruiting activity for the volunteer army.
- People who might support the change are expected to self-select into the effort without direct engagement.
- As a result, the volunteer army fails to form beyond the initial core.

## Countermove
- Identify which people or groups should be invited into the volunteer army and contact them directly.
- Treat recruiting as a real workstream rather than as a side effect of visibility.
- Review whether volunteer growth is happening by intention or only by accident.
