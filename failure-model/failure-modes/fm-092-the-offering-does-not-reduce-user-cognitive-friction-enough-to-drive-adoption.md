## Failure Mode Statement
The offering does not reduce user cognitive friction enough to drive adoption, so intended users do not recognize the platform as easy enough, familiar enough, or relevant enough to start using.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the team assumes that what feels obvious, simple, or normal from an infrastructure perspective will also feel clear and low-friction from the user's point of view.

## Mechanism
- The effort underestimates the gap between platform-team assumptions and user mental models.
- The platform does not lower the practical recognition burden enough for users to see how it fits their everyday work.
- As a result, intended users remain unreached because the platform still feels too foreign, unclear, or effortful to enter.

## Countermove
- Design for the user's actual mental model rather than for the provider's mental model.
- Ask whether the platform removes enough recognition and interpretation burden for new users to begin confidently.
- Treat high cognitive friction as an adoption reach failure, not only as a usability issue.
