## Failure Mode Statement
Expansion capacity is never explicitly secured, so the effort expects continued growth without committing the people, time, or support needed for it.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the organization likes the idea of building on early success, but does not convert that intent into protected capacity for the next stage of work.

## Mechanism
- Follow-on expansion is treated as a natural continuation rather than as something that needs fresh capacity decisions.
- The effort relies on leftover availability instead of securing continued support deliberately.
- As a result, the next phase exists as ambition without the operating capacity needed to sustain it.

## Countermove
- Secure explicit people, time, and support for the expansion phase rather than assuming it will inherit capacity automatically.
- Treat continued expansion as a new capacity commitment, not only as a logical continuation.
- Ask what has actually been protected for the next stage, not only what is hoped for.
