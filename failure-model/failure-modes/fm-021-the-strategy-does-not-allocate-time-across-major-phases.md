## Failure Mode Statement
The strategy does not allocate time across major phases of work, so there is no high-level agreement about what happens first, what follows, and how effort should be distributed over time.

## Context
This tends to emerge when people talk about strategy in directional terms, but avoid making commitments about sequencing, timing, or focus windows.

## Mechanism
- The group describes what it wants to achieve, but not how time should be spent across major phases.
- No one defines an initial period, follow-on period, or strategic pacing model for the work.
- As a result, execution decisions are made ad hoc instead of against a planned initiative path.

## Countermove
- Define the major phases of work and the intended order between them.
- Make the first time window explicit, even if later phases remain adjustable.
- Use high-level timing to force real prioritization rather than leaving everything simultaneously important.
