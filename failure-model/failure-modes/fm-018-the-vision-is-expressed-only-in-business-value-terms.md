## Failure Mode Statement
The vision is expressed only in business value terms, without translating why it matters to the people expected to execute or support it.

## Context
This tends to emerge when the team is strong at aligning upward to executive priorities, but does not reframe the vision into terms execution members can recognize in their own work.

## Mechanism
- The vision is described through business outcomes such as cost reduction, strategic efficiency, or organizational impact alone.
- The group assumes execution members will automatically care once the business value is stated.
- As a result, the vision sounds important in principle but does not become relatable at the working level.

## Countermove
- Ask what the vision changes for the people who have to execute, adopt, or support it.
- Restate business value in terms of local burden, improved leverage, reduced friction, or clearer trade-offs.
- Reject vision explanations that work only upward and not downward.
