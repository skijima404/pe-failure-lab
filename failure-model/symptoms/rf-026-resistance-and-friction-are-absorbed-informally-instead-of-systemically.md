## Symptom Statement
Resistance and friction are absorbed informally instead of systemically, so blockers are handled through individual effort, workarounds, or local endurance rather than durable structural change.

## Consequence
The organization appears to keep moving, but only because specific people are carrying the burden manually, which makes the effort fragile and exhausting.

## Observable Signals
- Certain individuals repeatedly absorb blockers through personal effort rather than through structural fix
- Resistance is handled case by case without changing the underlying condition
- When a blocker disappears, it is because someone pushed through it manually, not because the system changed

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to feel that progress depends on who is willing to carry pain, not on whether the organization is actually removing barriers.

## Structural Impact
- Heroic behavior substitutes for structural improvement
- Barrier patterns recur because underlying causes stay in place
- Fragility and burnout risk increase around the people doing the absorbing
