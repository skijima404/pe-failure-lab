## Symptom Statement
The barrier-removal effort loses operating capacity and attention, so blockers remain known but are no longer actively worked down.

## Consequence
The effort gradually shrinks in practical effectiveness because people become too busy, too tired, or too distracted to keep resolving the friction that blocks change.

## Observable Signals
- Barrier-removal tasks remain open while other work takes priority
- Known friction stays in place because no one has enough time or energy to keep pushing it
- When asked what barrier-removal work is actively moving, the answer is thin, stalled, or outdated

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to feel that the effort is still alive in principle, but no longer has enough attention behind it to keep improving conditions for action.

## Structural Impact
- Operational follow-through weakens
- Blockers accumulate faster than they are removed
- Momentum declines because action does not become easier over time
