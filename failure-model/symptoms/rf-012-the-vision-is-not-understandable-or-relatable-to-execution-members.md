## Symptom Statement
The vision is not expressed in a way that execution members can easily understand, reuse, or relate to their own work.

## Consequence
Even if the vision is valid, it does not shape execution behavior because the people expected to carry it cannot internalize it clearly enough to act on it.

## Observable Signals
- Execution members repeat the vision inconsistently or reduce it to slogans
- The vision is hard to connect to day-to-day choices, local trade-offs, or current work
- When asked what the vision means for their own team or decisions, answers stay vague

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to hear the vision as something leadership or strategy people believe in, but not as something that helps them execute differently.

## Structural Impact
- Strategic intent weakens as it moves downward into execution
- Reuse of the vision across teams becomes inconsistent
- The gap between strategic language and operational behavior widens
