## Symptom Statement
Delivered outputs do not create recognizable user success, so what was built cannot be honestly experienced or explained as a short-term win.

## Consequence
The team may ship something, but stakeholders do not experience a concrete success outcome, which makes the delivered result weak as proof of change value.

## Observable Signals
- Something is delivered, but users do not report a clear success experience tied to it
- The team can describe what was built, but not what user success it enabled
- When asked why the output counts as a win, the explanation stays at feature or artifact level rather than at outcome level

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to feel that work happened, but not that a meaningful win occurred for users or adopters.

## Structural Impact
- Output completion is mistaken for value demonstration
- User-centered evidence for the change remains weak
- Short-term wins lose credibility because they do not connect to lived success
