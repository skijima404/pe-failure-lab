## Symptom Statement
The coalition is formed, but it remains closed within one technical domain or one narrow team rather than spanning the groups needed for organizational change.

## Consequence
The effort is seen as a local team initiative, so surrounding groups do not become active collaborators and the coalition cannot create broader movement.

## Observable Signals
- Most coalition members come from the same team, function, or technical area
- Adjacent groups are informed about the effort but are not meaningfully represented in it
- When cross-functional buy-in is needed, the coalition has no credible internal bridge to those groups

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to interpret this as a platform-team effort asking for support, not as a shared cross-organizational coalition.

## Structural Impact
- Support remains thin outside the originating team
- Dependency on external goodwill increases because coalition representation is narrow
- The coalition cannot reliably influence cross-boundary decisions
