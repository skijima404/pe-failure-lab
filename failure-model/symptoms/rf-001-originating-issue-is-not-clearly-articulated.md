## Symptom Statement
The issue that triggered the change effort is not clearly articulated. When participants are asked why this initiative started, they cannot clearly explain the concrete problem, loss, pressure, or event that made change necessary.

## Consequence
The effort starts from a vague premise, so urgency remains weak and later decisions drift toward abstract solutioning without a shared problem definition.

## Observable Signals
- Participants talk about desired solutions, industry trends, or platform aspirations before naming the originating issue
- Explanations of why the effort started remain vague, inconsistent, or overly general
- When asked what concrete problem triggered the initiative, no one gives a clear answer

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to feel that the initiative sounds directionally reasonable or fashionable, but not grounded in a concrete problem that matters now.

## Structural Impact
- The change effort lacks a shared problem frame
- Priority decisions are harder to justify because the starting issue is unclear
- Later urgency-building and coalition-building rest on a weak foundation
