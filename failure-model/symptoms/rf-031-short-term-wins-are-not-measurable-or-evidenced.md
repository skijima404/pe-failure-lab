## Symptom Statement
Short-term wins are not measurable or evidenced, so the effort cannot show credible proof that a claimed win actually happened.

## Consequence
Wins remain arguable, easy to discount, or impossible to compare, which weakens their ability to justify continued investment or confidence.

## Observable Signals
- Claimed wins rely on assertion rather than on visible evidence or measurable change
- The team says something improved, but cannot show how improvement is known
- When asked how a win was validated, the answer is anecdotal, incomplete, or missing

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to feel that the effort may be generating value, but not in a way that can be confidently demonstrated or defended.

## Structural Impact
- Claimed wins are harder to trust or repeat
- Proof of progress stays weak in investment or governance discussions
- The effort loses leverage because evidence does not accumulate cleanly over time
