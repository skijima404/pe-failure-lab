## Symptom Statement
The volunteer army does not share a basic understanding of what Platform Engineering is, so people join with incompatible assumptions about the role, purpose, or expected value of the effort.

## Consequence
Even motivated participants pull in different conceptual directions, which makes onboarding, collaboration, and advocacy unstable from the start.

## Observable Signals
- Participants use the term Platform Engineering to mean materially different things
- Some people interpret the effort as a tool team, support team, DevOps service, SRE function, or portal project without shared correction
- When asked what Platform Engineering means in this effort, answers differ in role, scope, or purpose

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to feel that the effort is conceptually fuzzy, because the basic model of what they are joining is not shared.

## Structural Impact
- Onboarding becomes inconsistent
- Participation expands around conflicting mental models
- The effort spends energy resolving basic conceptual confusion instead of building momentum
