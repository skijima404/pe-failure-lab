## Symptom Statement
Short-term wins are not delivered in time, so the effort goes too long without visible proof that the change is working.

## Consequence
Stakeholders lose attention, patience, or belief because the effort keeps promising future value without producing timely evidence in the present.

## Observable Signals
- The first credible win is scheduled too far into the future
- Near-term delivery is repeatedly deferred in favor of bigger or more complete outcomes
- When asked what visible result will land soon, the answer points to a distant milestone instead of an imminent win

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to feel that the effort may eventually pay off, but not fast enough to sustain confidence or memory.

## Structural Impact
- Attention decays before value is demonstrated
- Investment justification weakens because the timeline feels too long
- The effort becomes vulnerable to being forgotten, deprioritized, or reframed as slow-moving overhead
