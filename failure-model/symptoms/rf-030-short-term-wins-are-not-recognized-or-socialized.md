## Symptom Statement
Short-term wins are not recognized or socialized, so even real progress fails to become shared organizational evidence.

## Consequence
The effort produces results that could strengthen belief and support, but those results do not travel far enough to reinforce momentum, legitimacy, or memory.

## Observable Signals
- A useful result exists, but many relevant stakeholders do not know it happened
- The meaning of the win is not communicated beyond the immediate team
- When asked what recent wins justify confidence, people struggle to name specific examples that have been shared broadly

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to feel that the effort may be doing useful work, but the organization does not reliably see or remember it.

## Structural Impact
- Wins fail to strengthen stakeholder belief at scale
- Recognition remains local instead of becoming organizational reinforcement
- Support and follow-on energy grow more slowly than the delivered results warrant
