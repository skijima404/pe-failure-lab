## Symptom Statement
People participate in the effort without shared urgency or belief, so involvement is driven more by obligation, compliance, or passive cooperation than by voluntary commitment.

## Consequence
The effort may gain attendance, but it does not gain real advocates who will carry energy, adoption, or reinforcement beyond formal participation.

## Observable Signals
- Members join activities out of duty but show little self-driven engagement
- Participation is present, but energy, advocacy, or initiative remain low
- When asked why they are involved, people describe obligation more often than belief or urgency

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to see the group as compliant but not truly bought in.

## Structural Impact
- Voluntary momentum remains weak
- Advocacy does not spread organically
- The effort depends on coordination pressure instead of self-reinforcing belief
