## Symptom Statement
The coalition exists, but it does not have enough leadership presence, decision authority, or political influence to move priorities across the organization.

## Consequence
The coalition can discuss and coordinate internally, but it cannot secure commitment, remove resistance, or shift organizational priorities when pressure rises.

## Observable Signals
- Coalition participants can describe the problem but cannot influence decisions beyond their local scope
- There is no member who can credibly advocate upward or across political boundaries
- When resistance appears, the coalition has discussion capacity but not decisive influence

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to see the coalition as well-intentioned but too weak to matter when real trade-offs or conflicts emerge.

## Structural Impact
- Coalition decisions lack organizational force
- Escalation paths remain weak or unavailable
- Change momentum depends on persuasion without sufficient authority behind it
