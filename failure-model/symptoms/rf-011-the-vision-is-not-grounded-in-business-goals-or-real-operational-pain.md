## Symptom Statement
The vision is defined, but it is not grounded in business goals or real operational pain that the organization is already experiencing.

## Consequence
The vision may sound coherent, but it lacks practical force because people cannot connect it to outcomes the business needs or to pain they are trying to relieve.

## Observable Signals
- The vision is described without clear connection to business goals, delivery friction, or organizational pain
- The language sounds strategic, but the current operational reason for the vision remains thin
- When asked what real business or delivery problem the vision is addressing, the answer is indirect or abstract

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to see the vision as conceptually reasonable but insufficiently anchored in real organizational need.

## Structural Impact
- The vision is harder to defend when trade-offs appear
- Strategic alignment remains shallow because the vision lacks a concrete organizational anchor
- The effort becomes more vulnerable to skepticism or drift
