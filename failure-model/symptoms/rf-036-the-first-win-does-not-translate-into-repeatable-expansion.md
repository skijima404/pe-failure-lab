## Symptom Statement
The first win does not translate into repeatable expansion, so the effort cannot turn one success into a pattern that works beyond the original case.

## Consequence
The organization learns that a success happened once, but not how to repeat it in other places, which limits acceleration, broader adoption, and confidence in further investment.

## Observable Signals
- The first win is treated as a one-off rather than as the start of a repeatable pattern
- Success cannot be transferred cleanly into a second team, case, or context
- When asked how the first win should be repeated elsewhere, the answer is unclear, overly local, or dependent on special conditions

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to feel that the initial success was real, but not yet reproducible enough to drive wider acceleration.

## Structural Impact
- Expansion stays constrained to the original case
- Learning does not convert into reusable change patterns
- Acceleration weakens because replication remains uncertain
- Continued investment becomes harder to justify because the win cannot be shown as repeatable leverage
