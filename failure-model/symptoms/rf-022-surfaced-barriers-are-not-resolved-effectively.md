## Symptom Statement
Barriers are surfaced, but they are not resolved effectively because ownership, authority, or removal action is too weak.

## Consequence
People learn that surfacing blockers does not reliably lead to change, which reduces trust in the barrier-removal effort and leaves friction in place.

## Observable Signals
- Reported blockers remain open without clear action or resolution
- The same barrier is discussed repeatedly without structural improvement
- When asked who is responsible for removing a barrier, the answer is vague, contested, or powerless

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to feel that the organization is aware of the blockers but not actually capable of removing them.

## Structural Impact
- Trust in escalation and resolution drops
- Barrier removal becomes symbolic rather than effective
- Friction remains in the system even after it is recognized
