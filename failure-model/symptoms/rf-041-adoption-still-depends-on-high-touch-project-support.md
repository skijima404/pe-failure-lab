## Symptom Statement
Adoption still depends on high-touch project support at the operating-model level, so each new case continues to require heavy team involvement instead of becoming cheaper and broader over time.

## Consequence
The team keeps expending heavy project-by-project effort to achieve usage, which prevents adoption from becoming cheaper, broader, and more normal over time.

## Observable Signals
- User acquisition and usage still require intensive direct support per project
- The support volume per adopting project does not decline meaningfully over time
- When asked whether adoption is becoming easier or lighter to support, the answer is weak or negative

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to feel that adoption is happening, but only because the team keeps pushing each case manually.

## Structural Impact
- The cost of adoption remains too high to institutionalize widely
- The change model stays dependent on continued intervention
- Growth remains constrained by support bandwidth
