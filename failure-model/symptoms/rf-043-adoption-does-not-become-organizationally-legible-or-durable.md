## Symptom Statement
Adoption does not become organizationally legible or durable outwardly, so even when usage exists it does not become widely recognized, reinforced, or stable enough across the organization to displace the old way.

## Consequence
The change remains reversible because the organization does not fully recognize its meaning, protect its legitimacy, or sustain its use over time.

## Observable Signals
- The platform is used in some projects, but its broader organizational significance is still unclear
- Users revert to old ways when support, pressure, or attention shifts
- When asked whether the new model has really become "the way we work now," the answer is doubtful or qualified

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to feel that the change exists, but not yet as something the organization clearly sees, values, and keeps.

## Structural Impact
- Regression to older ways remains likely
- Organizational recognition lags behind actual usage
- The change is not yet embedded as a durable norm
