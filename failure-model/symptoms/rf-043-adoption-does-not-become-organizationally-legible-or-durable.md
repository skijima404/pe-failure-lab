## Symptom Statement
Adoption does not become organizationally legible or durable, so even when usage exists it does not become stable enough outwardly across the organization or inwardly within the platform team to displace the old way.

## Consequence
The change remains reversible because the organization does not fully recognize its meaning, protect its legitimacy, or sustain its use over time, and the provider team does not internalize the new model strongly enough to anchor it.

## Observable Signals
- The platform is used in some projects, but its broader organizational significance is still unclear
- Users revert to old ways when support, pressure, or attention shifts
- The platform team itself continues to behave more like a delivery or support function than a durable platform product and adoption function
- When asked whether the new model has really become "the way we work now," the answer is doubtful or qualified

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to feel that the change exists, but not yet as something the organization clearly sees, values, keeps, and embodies through the provider team itself.

## Structural Impact
- Regression to older ways remains likely
- Organizational recognition lags behind actual usage
- Internal provider habits lag behind external rollout
- The change is not yet embedded as a durable norm
