## Symptom Statement
Adoption does not produce strong user satisfaction, so usage may occur but it does not create enough positive pull to support durable institutional change.

## Consequence
Users may comply or continue using the platform reluctantly, but they do not become strong advocates or stable repeat adopters.

## Observable Signals
- Users adopt the offering, but satisfaction remains muted, uneven, or fragile
- Feedback suggests that the platform is tolerated more than genuinely valued
- When asked whether users would choose the new way again, the answer is hesitant or mixed

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to feel that adoption has happened technically, but not in a way that creates durable preference.

## Structural Impact
- Retention and advocacy remain weak
- Institutionalization is less likely because satisfaction does not reinforce usage
- The platform remains easier to abandon when pressure rises
