## Symptom Statement
Short-term wins are not clearly defined, so the effort cannot tell what near-term result would count as meaningful proof of progress.

## Consequence
The team keeps working without a shared definition of what should be delivered, validated, or recognized as a win, which makes progress harder to design and defend.

## Observable Signals
- People use the word "win" loosely, but cannot state what concrete result would qualify
- Candidate wins are described as activity, output, or hope rather than as a validated outcome
- When asked what would count as a short-term win, the answer is vague, inconsistent, or shifts during discussion

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to feel that the effort wants quick wins in principle, but has not defined them tightly enough to guide decisions or prove value.

## Structural Impact
- Delivery choices are harder to prioritize around evidence-bearing results
- Validation and recognition become subjective
- The effort loses clarity about what near-term success is supposed to demonstrate
