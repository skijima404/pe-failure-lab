## Symptom Statement
People may join the effort, but they are not aligned to the actual vision and strategy that the change is supposed to advance.

## Consequence
Participation increases without increasing coherence, so the expanded group can pull the effort in conflicting directions rather than reinforcing the intended change.

## Observable Signals
- Different members describe the purpose of the effort in incompatible ways
- Participation grows, but not around a shared strategic direction
- When asked how their work supports the vision and strategy, answers diverge or stay generic

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to feel that people are joining activity, but not necessarily joining the same change.

## Structural Impact
- The effort gains participation without gaining alignment
- Strategic drift becomes more likely as more people join
- The platform team must spend more energy correcting direction rather than multiplying momentum
