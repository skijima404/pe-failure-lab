## Symptom Statement
The strategy does not define a credible path from vision to execution, because the major phases, sequence, and progression of work are not clearly established.

## Consequence
The team cannot translate the vision into an actionable sequence, so execution either stalls or jumps prematurely into reactive delivery work.

## Observable Signals
- The team can state the vision but cannot explain the major phases or sequence of initiatives
- There is no credible high-level path such as what should happen in the first months and what should follow after
- When asked what comes after vision-setting, the answer jumps directly into intake, delivery, or scattered requests

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to interpret this as a strategy gap rather than as a motivation problem, because the vision exists but the path does not.

## Structural Impact
- Vision and execution remain weakly connected
- The team becomes more vulnerable to premature demand-taking or scattered action
- Strategic intent is not converted into a usable change path
