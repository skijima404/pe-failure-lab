## Symptom Statement
Volunteer involvement grows, but it is skewed toward the wrong roles or communities, so the people needed to drive adoption or real change remain underrepresented.

## Consequence
The volunteer army may look active, but it does not spread influence where it matters, leaving key roles such as developers or other critical adopters insufficiently involved.

## Observable Signals
- Participation is concentrated in roles that observe, coordinate, or discuss, while key adopters or practitioners remain thin
- The same types of people keep joining, but missing roles do not become more represented over time
- When asked which critical roles are actually joining the volunteer effort, the answer reveals a narrow role mix

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to feel that the army is expanding numerically, but not expanding into the parts of the organization that would make the change real.

## Structural Impact
- Adoption leverage remains weak because important roles are not present in enough depth
- Advocacy spreads unevenly across the organization
- The volunteer army becomes broader in appearance than in strategic usefulness
