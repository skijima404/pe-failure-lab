## Symptom Statement
The volunteer army does not understand the vision and strategy clearly enough to explain them, apply them, or act consistently with them.

## Consequence
Participation remains shallow because people cannot turn the stated direction into confident advocacy, adoption, or action.

## Observable Signals
- Members repeat the vision or strategy inconsistently or incorrectly
- People participate in activities but cannot explain the strategic direction in their own words
- When asked what the change is trying to do, answers remain confused, partial, or contradictory

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to see this as a communication or onboarding problem rather than as resistance, because people are present but not strategically oriented.

## Structural Impact
- Strategic direction becomes weaker as it spreads
- Advocacy quality drops because supporters cannot explain the change clearly
- Adoption is more fragile because understanding is shallow
