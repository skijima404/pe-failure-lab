## Symptom Statement
In conversation, the case for change is framed mainly in terms of future problems or future benefits rather than present-day loss or pressure. The discussion does not establish a concrete issue that makes action necessary now.

## Consequence
Stakeholders may agree in principle, but they do not feel enough urgency to change priorities or commit resources in the present.

## Observable Signals
- Explanations begin with future-oriented concerns such as "we may need this later" or "this will become important"
- When asked "Why now?", the answer returns to future-state readiness rather than current pain, drag, or loss
- Participants accept the logic but defer action because the problem does not feel immediate

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to hear this as strategically reasonable but not time-critical.

## Structural Impact
- Transformation remains stuck behind immediate operational demands
- Urgency is weakened because present cost of inaction is not visible
- The effort is treated as future-oriented improvement rather than current organizational necessity
