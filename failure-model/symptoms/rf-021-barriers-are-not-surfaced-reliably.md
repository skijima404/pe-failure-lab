## Symptom Statement
Barriers are not surfaced reliably, so important blockers remain invisible, underreported, or unsaid until they have already slowed the change effort.

## Consequence
The effort keeps pushing forward without a usable picture of what is actually blocking action, which makes removal work reactive, incomplete, and inconsistent.

## Observable Signals
- People encounter friction repeatedly, but it is not captured in a durable way
- Known blockers remain informal, fragmented, or dependent on memory
- When asked what barriers are currently slowing adoption or action, the answer is incomplete or uncertain

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to feel that barriers exist everywhere, but no one has a trustworthy way to surface and track them.

## Structural Impact
- Barrier handling becomes ad hoc
- Shared visibility into blockers remains weak
- The effort loses leverage because blockers are not turned into actionable inputs
