## Symptom Statement
Role and responsibility boundaries are unclear, so teams cannot tell who should decide, build, enable, coordinate, or absorb specific kinds of work.

## Consequence
Work slows down because decisions, handoffs, and problem resolution become tangled in ambiguity about who is responsible.

## Observable Signals
- Teams disagree about who owns a given decision, task, or operating responsibility
- PO, developers, enabling teams, or platform teams overlap without clear boundary
- When asked who is accountable across team boundaries, the answer depends on interpretation rather than shared agreement

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to experience the effort as structurally confusing, because the work crosses boundaries that have not been made explicit.

## Structural Impact
- Ownership becomes disputed or diffuse
- Escalation paths weaken because responsibility is unclear
- Coordination cost rises across team and function boundaries
