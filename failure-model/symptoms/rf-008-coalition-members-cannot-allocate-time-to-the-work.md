## Symptom Statement
People are named as coalition members, but they cannot allocate enough time or attention to do the work required of the coalition.

## Consequence
The coalition exists nominally, but coordination stalls, decisions slip, and the effort loses momentum because members remain trapped by their primary delivery load.

## Observable Signals
- Members repeatedly join discussions late, delegate attendance, or cannot follow through on agreed actions
- Coalition work is treated as extra work rather than as protected change work
- When asked who is advancing a coalition task, the answer depends on whoever happens to have spare time

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to interpret this as a scheduling problem, but the deeper issue is that the coalition has not been given real operating capacity.

## Structural Impact
- Coalition continuity becomes unreliable
- Shared ownership degrades because members cannot sustain attention
- Time pressure pushes the coalition back into symbolic participation
