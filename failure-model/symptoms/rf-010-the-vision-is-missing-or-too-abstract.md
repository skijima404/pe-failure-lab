## Symptom Statement
The vision is missing, or it remains too abstract to guide real decisions about what Platform Engineering is trying to change.

## Consequence
People can talk about Platform Engineering in broad terms, but they cannot use the vision to make trade-offs, set direction, or explain what should actually happen next.

## Observable Signals
- Different people describe the vision in inconsistent or overly generic ways
- The vision sounds directionally positive but does not help resolve concrete decisions
- When asked what Platform Engineering is specifically trying to change, the answer stays vague

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to feel that a vision exists in name, but not in a form that is operationally useful.

## Structural Impact
- Decision criteria remain unstable
- Scope is more easily pulled in conflicting directions
- Strategic discussion drifts back into generic aspirations
