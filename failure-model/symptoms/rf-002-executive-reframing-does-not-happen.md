## Symptom Statement
In conversation, the initiative is not translated into executive decision language such as revenue, risk, cost, speed, or strategic execution. The discussion stays technical, and no clear business reframing is established.

## Consequence
Executive stakeholders do not develop enough urgency to allocate budget, headcount, or political support, so the initiative remains under-resourced and is displaced by nearer-term delivery demands.

## Observable Signals
- When asked "Why should leadership care?", the answer stays in tooling, architecture, or developer-experience language
- The discussion emphasizes technical concepts without connecting them to executive priorities
- Requests for budget or headcount are deferred because the business case is still not decision-ready

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to interpret this as a sponsorship problem, but the deeper issue is that the change has not been reframed in terms leadership uses to make priority and investment decisions.

## Structural Impact
- The initiative lacks the authority and resources needed to advance beyond local effort
- Momentum depends on volunteer effort or heroics rather than institutional backing
- The bridge between technical pain and executive action remains weak, making broader change fragile
