## Symptom Statement
The coalition is not genuinely owned from within the organization, and the practical center of direction remains dependent on external consultants, outsiders, or imported methods.

## Consequence
The coalition may appear active, but commitment, trust, and continuity remain weak because the change is not experienced as internally owned.

## Observable Signals
- External consultants lead core direction-setting, alignment work, or change messaging
- Internal members participate, but the visible center of gravity stays outside the organization or inside an imported playbook
- When asked who is truly carrying the coalition internally, the answer is unclear or overly dependent on external actors or methods

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to see the effort as externally driven support work or faithful method execution rather than as a coalition the organization itself is committed to building.

## Structural Impact
- Internal ownership remains underdeveloped
- Coalition legitimacy becomes fragile when external support is reduced
- The organization struggles to convert temporary guidance into sustained internal movement
