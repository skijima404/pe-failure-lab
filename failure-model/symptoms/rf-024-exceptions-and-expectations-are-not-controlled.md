## Symptom Statement
Exceptions and expectations are not controlled, so incoming requests, special cases, and surrounding assumptions begin to distort how the change effort operates.

## Consequence
The team spends increasing effort reacting to exceptions and expectation drift, which weakens consistency and makes action harder instead of easier.

## Observable Signals
- Exception requests increase without clear rules for handling them
- Stakeholders expect support, flexibility, or special treatment beyond what the effort intended to provide
- When asked how exceptions or expectation drift should be handled, the answer is unclear or improvised

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to feel that the effort is being reshaped by surrounding pressure faster than it can define its own operating boundaries.

## Structural Impact
- Operational consistency declines
- Boundary pressure increases
- Barrier removal effort is diverted into reactive expectation handling
