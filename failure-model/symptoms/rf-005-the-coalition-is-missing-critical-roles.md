## Symptom Statement
The coalition does not include the people or role types needed to move the change forward effectively.

## Consequence
Important work such as strategy, sponsorship, coordination, or trust-building remains uncovered, so the coalition becomes fragile and unable to sustain meaningful progress.

## Observable Signals
- Key gaps become visible when no one can speak credibly for strategy, management influence, cross-team coordination, or psychological safety
- The same small group is expected to cover too many different responsibilities
- When asked who is handling a critical coalition function, the answer is vague, overloaded, or absent

## User Interpretation
Participants are likely to see the coalition as incomplete or underpowered, even if a group has technically been assembled.

## Structural Impact
- Responsibility is concentrated in too few people
- Coalition resilience drops because critical functions depend on informal heroics
- Important conversations fail to happen because the right role is not present
