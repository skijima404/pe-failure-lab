## Principle Statement
Managers and executives should be engaged continuously so they reinforce legitimacy, protect capacity, shape expectations, and keep the effort visibly worth supporting.

## Why It Matters
Platform change weakens quickly when upper-layer audiences stop signaling that participation, adoption, and continued investment remain important.
Their ongoing engagement forms part of the continuity base that keeps people, budget, protected time, and organizational permission from eroding between visible wins.

## What This Principle Protects
- business legitimacy
- protected participation
- protected people and budget
- management reinforcement
- continued investment confidence
- organizational message-out

## Typical Failure Shape
Leaders notice the effort at the start or after a win, but do not keep turning that awareness into visible signals, protection, and reinforcement over time.
As a result, the continuity base weakens: people are reassigned, budgets become harder to defend, participation loses protection, and the effort becomes more fragile between milestones.
